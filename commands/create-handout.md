Create a student handout or worksheet for: $ARGUMENTS

## Steps

1. **Identify purpose** — practice, guided notes, comprehension check, homework, or scaffolded support. Infer from context or ask.
2. **Search curriculum** — use EasedUP MCP to find relevant content descriptions for this topic and year level
3. **Design for the student** — write in student-facing language appropriate for the year level
4. **Generate the resource** — write the complete handout as TipTap-compatible HTML, maximum 1–2 pages

## Required Sections

- **Handout Title**
- **Subject Area · Year Level · Date: _______ · Name: _______**
- **Curriculum Content Descriptions** — brief reference (teacher-facing, can be smaller or in a note at the bottom)
- **Learning Intention** — stated clearly for students ("By the end of this activity, I will be able to…")
- **Instructions** — clear, direct instructions for students
- **Content** — questions, tasks, or activities, which may include:
  - Multiple choice questions
  - Short answer questions
  - Sentence starters or scaffolds
  - Diagrams to label or complete
  - Tables to fill in
  - Open-ended questions
- **Response Space** — indicate where students write (e.g., lines, blank boxes, or `[________]` placeholders)

## Quality Guidelines

- Use language appropriate for the target year level
- Focus on one key learning outcome
- Include a mix of question types (recall, application, analysis)
- Keep it to 1–2 pages — concise and focused
- Leave adequate white space for student responses

Output the complete handout as HTML.
